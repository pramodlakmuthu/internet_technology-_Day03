Sri Lanka, officially the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka, is an island country in South Asia, located in the Indian Ocean. It is separated from India by the Gulf of Mannar and the Palk Strait and shares a maritime border with the Maldives to the southwest. The capital city is Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte, while Colombo is the largest city and the political, financial, and cultural center. Sri Lanka's population is approximately 22 million, with about 74% being Sinhalese and 15% Tamils. The country has a rich history, with evidence of prehistoric human settlement dating back 125,000 years. Sri Lanka has been a major trading hub and has a long history of international engagement and cooperation. It is a member of several international organizations, including SAARC, the G77, the Non-Aligned Movement, and the Commonwealth of Nations.
